Computer Science in Virginia

In 2017, the Virginia Department of Education published its first iteration of the Virginia Computer Science Standards of Learning. This site is all about those standards, how to teach them, and what resources educators can use to deepen their computer science knowledge.

CS Strands

The Virginia Computer Science SOLs are divided into six "strands", each of which deals with one aspect of computer science.

  • Algorithms & Programming

    In this strand, students learn how to code and how to apply computational thinking practices to the process of creating software.

  • Computing Systems

    In this strand, students learn about how computers work, how to troubleshoot computing systems, and what computers are made of.

  • Cybersecurity

    In this strand, students learn about how to keep themselves and their data safe in the digital world, including everything from digital hygiene to common cyberattacks and vulnerabilities.

  • Data & Analysis

    In this strand, students learn about how to use computing tools to collect, analyze, and communicate about data. This strand also encompasses a wide range of data science and AI/machine learning concepts.

  • Impacts of Computing

    In this strand, students learn about how computing impacts local, national, and global social contexts. Students investigate historical impacts of computing, make predictions about the future of computing, and explore careers in computing.

  • Networks & the Internet

    In this strand, students learn about how computers access, transmit, and receive data over local networks and the Internet. Students explore how to build networks, common networking issues, and the protocols that make up our modern networking infrastructure.
